### Abstract
Supply chains in agriculture, pharmaceuticals, food safety, e-commerce, and warehouse operations are often fragmented across disconnected systems, which reduces transparency, delays recall actions, and increases the risk of counterfeit products and data tampering. This project presents **ChainTrace**, a unified, multi-domain supply chain traceability platform that integrates blockchain, IoT simulation, analytics, and web-based verification into a single architecture. The system records product lifecycle events from origin to final delivery and anchors critical checkpoints to a blockchain network for tamper-evident traceability. To support repeated academic testing without transaction cost, the blockchain layer is configured on **Ganache** with **Hardhat** deployment and smart contract interaction.

The platform provides role-based modules for producers, distributors, retailers, inspectors, auditors, administrators, and consumers. It supports product registration, checkpoint updates, QR-based verification, dashboard analytics, and domain-adaptive workflows. Real-time capabilities are enabled through WebSockets and MQTT-based IoT event ingestion, while PostgreSQL serves as the primary data store for operational and reporting data. Redis is used for low-latency cache and event-oriented processing where required.

The proposed solution improves trust, accountability, and operational visibility across supply chain stages. It reduces manual reconciliation effort, strengthens compliance readiness, and enables faster issue isolation during recalls or anomaly events. ChainTrace demonstrates that a single extensible platform can support multiple industry domains while preserving traceability, scalability, and practical deployability. The implementation is suitable for academic evaluation and can be extended for enterprise-level integrations and production deployments.
